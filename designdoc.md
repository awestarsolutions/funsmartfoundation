# Fun Smart Foundation

# UI / UX Design Direction Document

**Project:** Premium NGO & CSR Website

**Purpose:** Design Reference for UI Creation

---

# Design Vision

The website should establish Fun Smart Foundation as a premium CSR implementation partner trusted by enterprises, government organizations, and institutions. It should feel refined, confident, and editorial rather than charitable or donation-focused.

The design should evoke the same confidence as a high-end consulting firm while remaining warm and human through authentic storytelling and photography.

**The first impression should be:**

> "This organization can execute CSR initiatives professionally at scale."

---

# Brand Personality

The visual identity should communicate:

* Professional
* Trustworthy
* Purpose-driven
* Modern
* Sophisticated
* Human
* Transparent
* Premium
* Calm
* Confident

Never feel playful or overly emotional.

---

# Overall Design Style

Imagine a blend of:

* Stripe's clean spacing
* Apple's editorial photography
* Linear's typography
* Notion's simplicity
* Goodera's structured information architecture

The design should avoid trends and instead focus on timeless visual quality.

---

# Visual Language

### Emphasize

* Large editorial photography
* Strong typography
* Spacious layouts
* Clean grids
* Elegant cards
* Thin borders
* Soft shadows
* Subtle animations
* Natural textures
* Minimal decorative elements

### Avoid

* AI-generated gradients
* Abstract blobs
* Floating 3D shapes
* Cartoon illustrations
* Overuse of icons
* Bright NGO colors
* Generic stock photos
* Excessive rounded shapes
* Glassmorphism
* Neumorphism

The design should feel intentionally crafted rather than generated.

---

# Color System

### Primary

Deep Forest Green (#16423C)

Represents sustainability, trust, and growth.

### Secondary

Slate Blue (#243447)

Used for navigation, headings, and premium accents.

### Accent

Emerald Green (#10B981)

Reserved for CTAs, highlights, and interactive states.

### Background

Warm Off White (#FAFAF7)

Avoid pure white across large sections.

### Cards

Pure White

### Borders

Light Neutral Gray

### Text

Near Black (#111827)

Use color sparingly. White space should carry the design.

Left:

Headline

Supporting paragraph

Primary CTA

Secondary CTA

Photography is the centerpiece of the website.

Preferred imagery:

* Corporate volunteering
* Tree plantation
* Education programs
* Healthcare camps
* Women entrepreneurship
* Community engagement
* Children learning
* Environmental restoration

Editing style:

Natural colors

Soft contrast

Real people

Documentary photography

Avoid:

Artificial smiles

Office handshakes

Perfectly posed groups

Stock models

The website should feel authentic.

# Iconography

Uniform stroke width.

Use icons only to support content, never as decoration.

# Motion Design

Animations should enhance usability, not attract attention.

Recommended:

Fade on scroll

Image reveal

Counter animation

Button hover

Section transitions

Avoid:

Parallax

Complex page transitions

Excessive motion

---

# Responsive Behaviour

Desktop first.

Tablet optimized.

Mobile should not simply stack sections; layouts should be redesigned where necessary.

Navigation becomes a full-screen menu.

Cards become horizontal where appropriate.

Images remain dominant.

---

# Accessibility

Minimum AA contrast.

Keyboard accessible.

Large touch targets.

Readable font sizes.

Proper semantic hierarchy.

---

# Image Direction

Approximately:

60% photography

20% typography

20% interface

The design should never rely on decorative graphics to fill empty space.

---

# Overall User Experience

Every interaction should reinforce professionalism and trust.

The experience should feel calm, premium, and deliberate. Visitors should perceive Fun Smart Foundation as an organization capable of delivering enterprise-grade CSR programs with transparency and measurable impact.

The website should leave users with the impression that they are engaging with a modern social impact organization that combines purpose with operational excellence—not a traditional NGO dependent on outdated web design conventions.
